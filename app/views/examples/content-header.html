A content header is the main header on every page. It's used in conjunction with breadcrumbs to inform users of what they're doing - and where they're doing it.

{{>mot_a_content-header CONTEXT_KEY}}

Components

The content header can contain up to 4 pieces of information.

  1. Type
    Which 'entity level' or 'task' are you in?

    • Authorised Examiner
    • Vehicle Testing Station
    • MOT Testing
    • Your Account

  2. Title
    What are you doing?

    • Managing something
    • Changing something
    • Viewing something (VTS name, AE name or Person name)
    • Reviewing something

  3. Progress (optional)
    Does the task have multiple steps? In the format 'Step n of total'.

  4. Tertiary (optional)
    Occasionally, a tertiary piece of information may be required to clarify a user's location within the site.
Headings should always be sentence case, with no full stops.
